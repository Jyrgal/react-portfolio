Mastercard Newsroom

Mastercard and Ethoca announce new support for retailers

Mastercard and Ethoca announced a new support package for all retailers in Australia and New Zealand to help tackle fraud and chargebacks.

Mastercard and Samsung Partner to Enable Digital Inclusion

Mastercard announced a global partnership with Samsung to enable digital access to consumers and small businesses in emerging markets through its extensible Pay on Demand platform.

Wellcome and Mastercard Launch Initiative

Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, Wellcome and Mastercard Launch Initiative to Speed Development and Access to Therapies for COVID-19

Path to Priceless Initiative

To reaffirm its commitment to women entrepreneurs, Mastercard is launching Path to Priceless to shine a light on their journey and provide a curated calendar of physical and digital advisement and mentorship opportunities.

Naomi Osaka launches the Breakthrough Room at Australian Open 2020

She’s known for her skills on the court but today tennis champion Naomi Osaka took on a competitive challenge with a difference – the Breakthrough Room – a new technology inspired experience by Mastercard.

Mastercard’s nationwide search for young inventors

Mastercard launched a nationwide search for aspiring young inventors, inviting them to undertake a Mastercard Innovation Challenge to come up with a new invention or idea that uses technology to make the world a better place.

Mastercard Increases Contactless Payment Limit Across Australia

Mastercard has announced that contactless payment limits in Australia will increase from AU$100 to AU$200 enabling more transactions to take place without the need to touch a PIN pad.

Mastercard evolves its brand mark by dropping its name

The Mastercard Symbol will now stand on its own across cards using the red and yellow brand mark, acceptance  marks at retail locations both in the physical and digital worlds, and major sponsorship properties.

Mastercard breaks down barriers and rallies neighbours together

Mastercard is breaking down the barriers between neighbours in one street in Melbourne, by replacing fences with tennis nets.

Mastercard enables contactless payments across Sydney trains

Commuters can now use their debit and credit card or contactless devices on the Sydney Trains network and NSW TrainLink Opal services.

Mastercard taps into the future of transport ticketing in Australia

Commuters on the F1 Manly to Circular Quay ferry can now use their contactless Mastercard cards or Mastercard enabled digital wallets to tap on at ferry gates.

Mastercard announces Cricket Australia partnership

A Priceless Summer of Sport awaits thanks to Mastercard’s existing Tennis Australia sponsorship and the latest three-year partnership that sees Mastercard become the Official Payment Provider of Cricket Australia.

Mastercard gives Fitbit users tap and go convenience

Fitness enthusiasts can now enjoy a wallet free workout, with Mastercard adding payment capabilities to the new Fitbit Ionic™.
